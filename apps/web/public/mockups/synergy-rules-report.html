▲ Rule 1: Shift Targets

Category: Direct (pair-specific)  •  Direction: Bidirectional  •  Score range: 3 – 10

In Lorcana, Shift lets you play a character on top of another character, paying the Shift cost instead of the full ink cost. The shifted character skips the drying phase (it can act immediately), making Shift one of the strongest tempo plays in the game. Three variants exist: Standard (same name), Classification (e.g., Puppy Shift targets Puppy characters), and Universal (targets any character).

Standard Shift (Gap-Based Scoring)

The key metric is curve gap: shiftCost − baseCost. A gap of 1 means "play base on turn N, Shift next turn" — the ideal pattern.

Score Tier Gap Inkwell Explanation
9 Strong 1 Both inkable Perfect curve: play base on turn N, Shift next turn. Both cards are inkable as fallback.
8 Strong 1 One inkable Perfect curve: play base on turn N, Shift next turn. One card is inkable as fallback.
7 Strong 1 Neither On curve but neither card is inkable — less flexible if drawn off-curve.
7 Strong 2 Any Smooth curve: base flows naturally into Shift within a couple of turns.
5 Moderate 0 Any Same cost — no ink savings from Shifting, but skips the drying phase.
5 Moderate 3 Any Wide 3-turn gap — playable but slow to set up.
3 Weak 4+ Any Poor alignment — the cost gap makes it hard to shift on tempo.
3 Weak < 0 Any Base costs more than Shift — no practical reason to shift "down".

Why does inkwell only matter for gap=1? At the ideal curve, the difference between "good" and "great" is draw flexibility. Inkable cards can be placed face-down as ink — they're never dead draws. At wider gaps, the curve itself is the limiting factor, not draw flexibility.

Free Shift (Shift 0, Cost-Based Scoring)

Free Shift uses a completely different scoring path because the normal gap math breaks down — every gap would be negative. Instead, scoring is based on how easy it is to get the base card into play first.

Score Tier Base Cost Condition? Explanation
10 Perfect ≤ 3 Yes Free Shift onto cheap base + base enables the Shift condition. The dream combo.
9 Strong ≤ 3 No Free Shift: Play base early, Shift for 0 ink. Extremely powerful.
8 Strong 4 – 5 Yes Free Shift onto mid-cost base + condition enabler in one card.
7 Strong 4 – 5 No Free Shift but the base takes until mid-game to deploy.
6 Moderate 6+ Yes Free Shift but expensive base — hard to deploy. Condition helps.
5 Moderate 6+ No Free Shift but expensive base — hard to get into play first.
Example — Score 10 (Free Shift + Condition Activation)

Score 10 — Free Shift: Play Anna - Little Sister early, then shift into Anna - Soothing Sister for 0 ink. Anna - Little Sister is both a Shift target and enables the free Shift condition.

Condition Matcher System

Some cards have conditional Shift — they only gain the Shift keyword when a specific game condition is met. The condition matcher system detects whether a base card's abilities can satisfy that condition, adding a +1 bonus (capped at 10).

Discard Condition
Looks for
card left a player's discard
Satisfied by

Extending the system: New matchers can be added to SHIFT_CONDITION_MATCHERS in rules.ts. Each matcher pairs a condition regex (checked against the Shift card) with a satisfiedBy regex (checked against the base card).

Shift Variants

Beyond the standard same-name Shift, two additional variants change which characters are valid targets. All variants use the same scoring function (curve gap, inkwell flexibility, free Shift tiers).

Variant Keyword Valid Targets Example Card
Standard Shift N Characters with the same base name Elsa - Ice Maker (Shift 4)
Classification X Shift N Characters with the specified classification (e.g., Puppy) Thunderbolt - Wonder Dog (Puppy Shift 3)
Universal Universal Shift N Any character Baymax - Giant Robot (Universal Shift 4)

Ink compatibility: Dual-ink cards (like Thunderbolt — Amber/Sapphire) lock the deck to exactly those two ink colors. The engine pre-filters all synergy results to only show ink-compatible cards. So Puppy Shift on Thunderbolt only shows Amber and Sapphire Puppy characters, and Universal Shift on Baymax (Emerald/Sapphire) only shows Emerald and Sapphire characters.

◆ Rule 2: Lore Loss

Category: Playstyle (density-based)  •  Playstyle: Lore Steal  •  Direction: Bidirectional  •  Uniform score: 7

Cards that make the opponent lose lore all reinforce the same denial strategy. Value comes from density — having 6-8 lore-loss cards in a deck makes the strategy consistent. No individual pair is a core combo; it's the accumulation that makes the archetype.

Score Tier Pairing Explanation
7 Strong Any two lore-loss cards Both cards make the opponent lose lore — they reinforce the same denial strategy.

Matches any card with text matching: opponents? loses? (\d+ )?lore

Example — Lore Steal Pair
Thievery
Cost: 1 Action Ruby
"Chosen opponent loses 1 lore. Gain 1 lore."
↔

Jasmine - Rebellious Princess
Cost: 3 Character Ruby
"Whenever this character quests, each opponent loses 1 lore."
Score 7 — Both Thievery and Jasmine - Rebellious Princess make the opponent lose lore.

■ Rule 3: Location Control

Category: Playstyle (density-based)  •  Playstyle: Location Control  •  Direction: Bidirectional  •  Score range: 3 – 7

Location synergies are built around 8 roles that cards can play in a location strategy. The engine detects each role via text pattern matching, then scores pairs based on how the roles interact.

Location Roles

Role Score Tier What it does Example
At-Payoff 7 Strong Gets bonuses when characters are at a location Elsa - Ice Artisan
Play Trigger 7 Strong Activates effects whenever you play a location Elsa - Ice Artisan
Buff 7 Strong Strengthens locations with resist, protection, or stat boosts Fix-It Felix Jr.
Location Ramp 7 Strong Reduces the cost of playing or moving to locations Elsa - Concerned Sister
Move 5 Moderate Moves characters to locations for positioning Transport Pod
In-Play Check 5 Moderate Gains benefits when you have locations in play John Silver
Tutor 5 Moderate Searches your deck or discard for location cards The Islands I Pulled from the Sea
Boost 5 Moderate Works with the Boost keyword to power up locations Webby's Diary

Cross-Synergy (Support + Support)

When two location-support cards have complementary roles (one enables what the other needs), they get a cross-synergy score. Same-role pairs and non-complementary pairs get no synergy.

Score Tier Condition Example
5 Moderate Both have high-value complementary roles Elsa (at-payoff) + Felix (buff)
3 Weak Complementary but not both high-value Transport Pod (move) + Islands (tutor)
— None Same role or non-complementary pair Two at-payoff cards

Anti-location exclusion: Cards that banish or remove locations (e.g., "banish chosen location") are automatically excluded from all location roles, even if their text otherwise matches a role pattern.
